# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BlakPath serves authorised Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander organisation
staff who receive, review and administer Confirmation of Aboriginality
applications, plus applicants and representatives completing public forms or
verifying certificates. Staff may be non-technical and work with sensitive,
trauma-associated records under time pressure. Their primary job is to record
information supplied by people, coordinate human review and preserve a clear,
auditable history without the software judging identity.

## Product Purpose

Provide a secure, community-controlled case-management and evidence workspace
that makes applications easier to receive, find and progress while enforcing
tenant isolation, human decision-making, respectful communication and
Australian data sovereignty. Success means authorised people can complete the
whole workflow confidently, while BlakPath never scores, ranks, predicts,
infers, approves or rejects a person's Aboriginality.

## Brand Personality

Calm, grounded and respectful. The product should feel clear and human to
non-technical users, trustworthy around highly sensitive information and
supportive without becoming sentimental or bureaucratic.

## Anti-references

Do not use sacred or ceremonial motifs, pseudo-Indigenous patterns, generic
corporate-government styling, gamified case management, opaque algorithmic
signals or decorative SaaS conventions that compete with the work. Avoid copy
or visual treatment that implies BlakPath has authority to determine identity.

## Design Principles

- Keep human authority visible: the system records and supports decisions but
  never makes or implies them.
- Make the next safe action obvious to a person who does not know the system.
- Treat privacy, tenant context and evidence state as understandable parts of
  the interface, not hidden implementation details.
- Use calm hierarchy and plain Australian English to reduce cognitive and
  emotional load.
- Prefer familiar, keyboard-operable product patterns with explicit feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA across public, applicant and staff journeys. Support keyboard
and screen-reader use, visible focus, text scaling, reduced motion, sufficient
contrast and status communication that never relies on colour alone. Validate
critical journeys with automation and assisted usability review involving
representative non-technical users.
